Number System

20/04/2021


1) Natural Number :-
  Natural Numbers are counting numbers Starts from 1,2,3... are the most familiar numbers. They are involved symbolically represent by "N" , sum of two natural number is natural number . The product of two natural is natural number.

2) Whole Number :-
   Natural number including with 0 (zero) is known as Whole Number .

3) Integer :-
   Natural number are subset of integers. Natural number without zero are called Integer .

4) Rational Number :-
   A fraction with an integer numerator & positive integer in the denominator it is represented in the form of p/q where , 'p' is integer and 'q' is non-zero integer or is not equal to zero .
An integer are rational number . Decimal number which have repeating pattern will also fall under this category .
eg. 0.83333 = 1/12

5) Irrational Number :-
   Irrational number that cannot be written as fraction i.e. p/q .
It is never ending number & does not repeat itself in the decimal form .
eg. π = 3.14159...

6) Real Number :-
   The set of Rational & Irrational number are called Real Number . Real Number represented by Symbol "R".

7) Prime Number :-
   Any Real Number that is divisible by 1 & itself .
eg. 2,3,5,7,9,11,13,17...

8) Even Number :-
   Natural number which is divisible by only 2 , are Even Number.
eg. 2,4,6,8...

9) Odd Number
   Natural number which is not divisible by 2 , are called Odd Number

1) Divisibility of 2 :-

 The Number of Divisible by 2 if it's unit digit (last digit) is divisible by any of " 0 , 2 , 4 , 6 , 8 " .

  Ex.   58694  is divisible by 2 .
         86945  is not divisible by 2.

3) Divisibility by 9 :-

  A number is divisible by 11 if the difference between the sum of its digit at odd places & sum of it's even places is either zero or divisible by 11 .

#1 ) Decimal Fraction :-
 Fraction in which denominator are powers of 10 are known as decimal fraction .

1/100 = 1 Ten'th = 0.1
1/100 = 1 Hundred'th = 0.01
99/100 = 99 Hundred'th = 0.99
7/1000 = 7 Thousand'th = 0.007

#2 ) Conversion of decimal into vulgar :-
 Put 1 in the denominator under the decimal point & end with it , as many zero as is the number of digit after the decimal.

    Ex. 0.25 = 25/100
        2.008 = 2008/1000

#3. a )  Annexing zeros to the extreme right of decimal fraction does not changes value .
        0.8 = 0.80 , 0.800 = 0.8000 etc.

#3. b ) If the numerator & denominator of a fraction contain the same number of decimal places , then remove the decimal sign.
        1.84 / 2.99 = 184 / 299
        0.365 / 0.584 = 365 / 584

#4 ) Operation on decimal Fraction :-

   A ) Addition & Subtraction of decimal fraction :-
     The given numbers are also place under place that a decimal points lies in one column . The numbers so arrange can now be added ot Subtracted in the usual way .

   B) Multiplication of decimal fraction by power of 10 :-
    If the decimal points to the right by as many places as is the power of ten.

    Ex . 5.9632 * 100 = 596.32
    Ex . 7.3212 * 10 = 73.212

   C) Multiplication of Decimal Fraction :-
    Multiply the given number considering them without the decimal point , Now in the product , the decimal point is must of to obtain as many places of decimal as the sum of the number of decimal number in given number .

     Ex. 0.2 * 0.02 * 0.002 =
     = (2*2*2 = 8 )
     sum of decimal places = 1+2+3 = 6
     = 0.0000008

     Ex. 1.2 * 0.02 * 0.0004 =
     = (12*2*4 = 96 )
     sum of decimal places = 1+2+4 = 7
     = 0.000000096

   D) Dividing decimal fraction by a counting number :-
    Divide the given number without considering the decimal point , by given counting number . Now in the quotient put the decimal point even as many places as there in the dividend .

     Ex. 0.0204/17 =
     204 / 17 = 12
     = 0.0012

     Ex. 22.5 / 5 =
     255/5 = 51
     = 5.1

   E) Dividing a decimal fraction by decimal fraction :-
    Multiply both dividend & devisor by suitable power of 10 to make devisor whole number .

   F ) Comparison of fraction :-
     Suppose some fraction or to be arranged in ascending or descending of magnitude then convert each of the fraction in the decimal form on arrange them according to following steps,

Eg,
   3/5 , 6/7 , 7/9
   Suppose we have to arrange all these three in descending order.
3/5 = 0.600
6/7 = 0.857
7/9 = 0.777
0.857 > 0.777 > 0.600


   G ) Recurring Decimal :-
     If in decimal fraction a figure or set of figure is repeated continuously then such number is called recurring decimal . In recurring decimal if figure is repeated it is expressed by putting bar on the set .
Eg,
     1/3 = 0.3333333... = 0.3

# BODMAS :

  This rule deficits the correct sequence in which the operations are to be executed. So as to find out the value of given expression.
   Here ,
     B stands for Bracket
     O stands for Of
     D stands for Division
     M stands for Multiplication
     A stands for Addition
     S stands for Subtraction

  Thus, in simplifying an expression, first of al the bracket must be removed strictly under ( ) , { } and [ ] after removing bracket, we must use the following operation strictly in the order,
    1) Of
    2) Division
    3) Multiplication
    4) Addition
    5) Subtraction
